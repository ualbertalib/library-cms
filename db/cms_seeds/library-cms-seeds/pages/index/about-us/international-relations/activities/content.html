Recent International Activities

In addition to the activities undertaken by members of our Library International Relations Office (LIRO) team, many other University of Alberta Librarians participate in some aspect of our programs, working locally with our visitors or travelling abroad. Some recent activities are listed below:

2015

  • Our Discovery Services Librarian and our Director of External Relations visited Cần Thơ University in Cần Thơ, Vietnam, to present workshops on RDA, and copyright and plagiarism.

2014

  • Our Director of External Relations and LIRO team leader was seconded to the Aga Khan University in Nairobi, Kenya and Karachi, Pakistan, to work on two projects: facilitating a strategic planning process for the library, and helping to set up the copyright office for the University.
  • The Collections Librarian for our Health Sciences Library presented a workshop on e-resources management for AGA Khan University librarians at the AKU Nairobi campus in Kenya.
  • Our Director of External Relations and LIRO team leader was the keynote speaker at the "2014 LIS Research Symposium: Research Data Management and Teaching Future LIS Professionals" held at the Florida Campus of the University of South Africa.
  • Our Discovery Services Librarian and our Digitization Librarian were invited presenters at the "59th All India International Conference of the Indian Library Association" held at the Indian Institute of Technology in Roorkee, India. While there, they met with IIT Roorkee librarians to discuss joint digitization and preservation projects.

2013

  • Our Liaison Librarian for Medicine travelled to Nairobi, Kenya to lead a workshop on Research Methods for Publication for librarians from Aga Khan University campuses in several countries. This workshop resulted in the submission of an article for publication in a scholarly journal.
  • A librarian from the Digital Services Centre, University of the West Indies, St. Augustine, Trinidad and Tobago, visited us to work with UofA librarians to learn about our metadata and digitization workflows, institutional repository policies and workflows, use of various metadata schemas, and metadata for special and rare collections.
  • Four UofA Librarians were invited to participate in the first "Indo-Canadian Library Best Practices Symposium" held at the Indian Institute of Technology in Roorkee, India. Our librarians discussed a potential partnership for digitizing some unique library resources at IIT Roorkee.
  • Our UofA Libraries Director of External Relations began working with the library of Cần Thơ University in Cần Thơ, Vietnam to develop an Interlibrary Loan program for electronic resources.
  • Three librarians from the Beijing Dance Academy visited the UofA Libraries: their Chief Librarian, Acquisitions and Cataloguing Librarian, and Technology Librarian. They met with our Chief Librarian and our Music and Fine Arts Liaison Librarians, toured the Rutherford Humanities & Social Sciences Library, viewed our digitization projects, and discussed our book exchange partnership and future librarian exchanges.

2012

  • Our Metadata and Cataloguing Librarian spent two weeks working with cataloguers at the University of the West Indies in Cave Hill, Barbados on an intensive metadata project to develop a structure to support their extensive digitized postcard collection.
  • The Head Librarian from our Augustana Campus Library travelled to Nairobi, Kenya to lead a second Information Literacy workshop for librarians from Aga Khan University campuses in several countries. These 2011 and 2012 workshops resulted in the development of interchangeable teaching modules that can be used in all of the AKU campus libraries.
  • Our Library International Relations Office began working with Chonnam National University in Gwangju, South Korea, to develop a Canadian Literature collection for their library.
  • At the request of the Principal, University of the West Indies, Mona, Jamaica, our UofA Libraries Director of External Relations led a team evaluating their library collections and services.

2011

  • The Head Librarian from our Augustana Campus Library travelled to Nairobi, Kenya to lead an Information Literacy workshop for librarians from Aga Khan University campuses in several countries.
  • Our UofA Libraries Director of External Relations implemented a program to develop the Nursing collection at the University of Ghana Library to support a teaching and research partnership between the University of Alberta Faculty of Nursing and the University of Ghana Faculty of Nursing.
