The Digital Scholarship Centre offers U of A students, staff, and faculty access to gaming consoles and our Virtual Reality (VR) Studio, These technologies can be used for learning, testing, and experiencing VR content.

VIRTUAL REALITY STUDIO

The DSC has one Valve Index VR system, an industry-leading VR kit that offers a high-definition display and advanced motion tracking.

VR Studio Policies

  1. First-time VR users must check in with staff for a brief overview of best practices, and how to select and use software.

  2. No food or drink in the studio.

  3. Do not remove equipment from the room.

  4. Users can book up to 4 hours per day.

  5. Room capacity: 4 People.

    6. BOOK THE VIRTUAL REALITY STUDIO: 2-20E1

    GAMING STUDIO

    The DSC is an academic centre designed for hands-on learning. We are pleased to offer a gaming space for those wanting to experience and learn video games.

    The gaming studio has a small selection of games for three video game consoles: a Nintendo Switch, a PlayStation 5 and an XBox Series S.

    Gaming Studio Policy

    1. This space is not intended to be used as study space: Study rooms are available on the main and basement levels of Cameron Library and other library locations.

      No food or eating in the studio; drinks with lids are permitted.

    2. Users can book up to 2 hours per day.

    3. Room capacity: 5 People.

    BOOK THE GAMING STUDIO: 2-20N

    GAME DEVELOPMENT SOFTWARE

    The DSC provides access to a variety of virtual reality and game development software, and includes Autodesk Maya, Terragen 4, Unity, and Unreal Engine.
