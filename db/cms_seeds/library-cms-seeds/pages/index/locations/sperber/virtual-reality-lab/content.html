Sperber Library - Virtual Reality Lab

Location: Basement of the Geoffrey & Robyn Sperber Health Sciences Library (L1-150A)

Hours of operation: 12:30-5:30 pm Monday to Friday

Book the VR Lab

The Sperber Virtual Reality (VR) Lab, is a versatile space for learning, experimentation, and immersive content exploration for all University of Alberta students, staff and faculty.

The Lab includes:

  • Dell Alienware Aurora R15 Workstation
    • Processor: 13th Gen Intel(R) Core(TM) i9-13900KF, 3000 Mhz, 24 Core(s), 32 Logical Processor(s)
    • RAM: 64 GB
    • Video: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090, 24 GB video RAM
    • C: Drive - 2TB NVME M2 SSD
    • D: Drive - 4TB Sata SSD

  • Dell 32” 4K UHD Gaming Monitor

  • Valve Index VR Kit configured via Steam client - includes controllers, base station, and charging cables
  • Meta Quest Pro configured via Meta Occulus - includes controllers, stylus tips, charging dock, protective cover and partial light blocker
  • 76-inch LCD Display
  • Cleanbox CX1 Ultraviolet Cleaning Station
  • Soft seating for 4 additional people

For more information contact sperberhelp@ualberta.ca

VR Lab Policy

  • Bookable by the University of Alberta, faculty, staff, and students (CCID will be required) for academic, non-commercial activities
  • 2 hour maximum bookings per person during VR Lab hours
  • Maximum occupancy – 5 people
  • No food or drink in the VR Lab
  • Equipment is the responsibility of the user and cannot be removed from the room
  • Occupants must ensure the room remains clean and tidy

VR at the U of A Library

This VR Lab is one of two main VR sites within the U of A Library System, the other is located at the Digital Scholarship Centre on the 2nd floor of Cameron Library. Visit the DSC VR Studio here.
