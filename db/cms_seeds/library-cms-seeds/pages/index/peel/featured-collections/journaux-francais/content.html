Journaux Français

Visit the French Language Newspapers Collection »

This collection includes full or partial runs of French-language newspapers. Some titles are long-running full-size newspapers, which offer invaluable accounts of the francophone communities living on the Prairies over time. Others are short-lived, often founded for political purposes, sometimes even by individual politicians to promote their own campaigns.

This collection includes La Survivance, the weekly official publication of l'Association canadienne-française de l'Alberta. This was the main weekly newspaper of the French community in Alberta, continued under the title Le Franco-Albertain and later as Le Franco.

The full 100-year run of La Liberté, a weekly Manitoba French-language newspaper which merged with the Saskatchewan newspaper Le patriote de l'Ouest to form La Liberté et le patriote in 1941. By 1971, it was known as La Liberté again. This project was digitized in partnership with the Société historique de Saint-Boniface, who coordinated this project; the Société franco-manitobaine and the Université de Saint-Boniface, who contributed funding for this project; and the Manitoba Legislative Library, who provided microfilmed copies for digitization.
