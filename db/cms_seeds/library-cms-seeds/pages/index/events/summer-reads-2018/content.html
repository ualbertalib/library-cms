Summer Reads - Libraries Staff Picks 2018

It is becoming a bit of a tradition here at the University of Alberta Libraries to present some suggestions for leisure reading at the height of summer. When polled this time around, our Libraries staff did not disappoint. The reading suggestions poured in and in great variety.

This year's list includes thrillers, mysteries, romance, graphic novels, non-fiction, and poetry. To learn more about a title, just click on the book cover to get a direct link to the book's record in the library catalogue. With the warm weather and fresh breeze blowing on the Quad, place a hold on one of these gems today, pick it up at the library that is most convenient for you and enjoy!

Once and forever: the tales of Kenji Miyazawa

By Miayazawa, Kenji (translated)

Kenji's stories have had great influence on the Japanese way of thinking in very subtle ways. Often seen as children's stories, they are anything but.

Motherhood

By Sheila Heti

It sparks a most deep and thoughtful conversation on why or why not women should become mothers.

Katlin Street

By Magda Szabó

Keenly and deeply illustrates the impact of past events on our lives.

Devotions: Selected poems

By Mary Oliver

Passionate and perceptive poems of the natural world and how precious it is.

Sourdough

By Robin Sloan

One word: Bread

Weapons of Math Destruction: How Big Data Increases Inequality and Threatens Democracy

By Cathy O'Neil

Illuminating and disturbing. Predictive algorithms are silently shaping and controlling our destinies from everything such as getting a job to finding a spouse.

Murder on the Orient Express

By Agatha Christie

Classic murder mystery! You won't know who done it unless you've seen the movie.

I like You: Hospitality Under the Influence

By Amy Sedaris

Hilarious and light hearted hospitality tips, recipes and home decor ideas!

Cultivating engaged staff: better management for better libraries

By Margaret Zelman Law

The intersection of anecdotes and theory

The vaccination picture

By, Timothy A. Caulfield

A quick read about vaccination with relevant illustrations from local artists

Birdie

By Tracey Lindberg

dark, touching, but I am not sure if I really know what happened in it!

At Home: A Short History of Private Life

By Bill Bryson

A very engaging and friendly exploration on the history of homes (mostly from an Occidental perspective). Bryson explores every room in his house while questioning the origins what we usually we take for granted.

You Too Can Have a Body Like Mine

By Alexandra Kleeman

The best thing about this novel is the way the author pulls you into a world that is just like ours but also just slightly off creating a strangeness that feels really creepy at times. Kleeman's story explores themes of appetite, consumption and satisfaction in all forms imaginable.

Less

By Andrew Sean Greer

A failing novelist travels around the world to avoid going to his ex-boyfriend's wedding - a humorous, fun, inspiring, & lovely story :)

The Custodian of Paradise

By Wayne Johnston

You will be drawn in by the main character, Sheilagh Fielding, and transported to a different world.

The Opposite of Loneliness

By Marina Keegan

Half short stories and half essays, this book gorgeously captures how it feels to be young and on the verge of adulthood, with all its hopes and fears.

Come, Thou Tortoise

By Jessica Grant

It's a quirky, clever, and heartfelt novel that is full of puns. Part of it is told from the perspective of a tortoise!

The Handmaid's Tale

By Margaret Atwood

How this book is still incredibly relevant (especially now) over 30 years following its original publication.

Barbe bleue

By Amélie Nothomb

A modern retelling of the classic "fairy tale", this short novel is an explosion of colours and flavours. You can practically taste the words.

Attached

By Amir Levine

Bringing Columbia Home

By Leinbach, Michael D., and Ward, Jonathan H.

The incredible number of people who worked together to recover what they could from this tragedy.

Boy, Snow, Bird

By Helen Oyeyemi

His Bloody Project: Documents Relating to the Case of Roderick Macrae

By Graeme Macrae Burnet

A bloody triple murder in the Scottish highlands in 1869. A small village and power dynamics. Possible insanity and at least one mutilated sheep. What more do you need for a summer read?

Believing is not the Same as Being Saved

By Lisa Martin

This book of poetry is a gorgeous meditation on faith and a life lived with loving attention. (Also, its author is from Edmonton, and the book was published by the University of Alberta press.)

The Troop

By Nick Cutter

A horror tale on Prince Edward Island!

The Sweetness at the Bottom of the Pie

By Alan Bradley

Great mystery writer! First book in a series.

Lost Japan

By Alex Kerr

Unique perspective on Japan that dispels some myths and really makes one want to visit (even if you have visited many times already).

The White Tiger

By Aravind Adiga

So many twists and completely engaging.

Speak

By Laurie Halse Anderson, Emily Carroll (Illustrator)

Graphic novel adaptation of the 1997 YA classic. The amazing art style adds even more depth and power to the original story #MeToo

Call Me by Your Name

By André Aciman

An intense gay love story set in 1980s Italy during one summer. The movie adaptation came out last year and is also in the catalogue.

Tin Man

By Sarah Winman

Short, sad, and beautiful - a nostalgic story about boyhood friends who grow into more and the decisions they make for their lives.

Big Little Lies

By Lianne Moriarty

Queen of the Tearling

By Johansen, Erika

The rise of the queen and her backstory to the throne.

The Invention of Wings

By Sue Monk Kidd

Women empowerment in a time of slavery.

A Landscape Blossoms Within Me

By Eeva Kilpi, translated by Donald Adamson

Finnish poet Eeva Kilpi, a nobel prize nominee, writes free verse poetry that explores everything from love, to nature, to aging. With great economy and simplicity her words convey profound heartbreak, whimsy and sarcasm.
