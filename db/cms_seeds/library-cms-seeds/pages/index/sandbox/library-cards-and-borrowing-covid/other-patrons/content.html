Borrowing Information (During Covid-19)

Extension Students | St. Stephen's Students | U of C Social Work Students | Students from other Canadian Post-Secondary Institutions | The Alberta Library | Alumni | Members of the Public

Borrowing Information for Extension Students

  • Returning certificate/diploma program/English Language School students: please contact library staff to have your borrowing privileges updated for the current academic year.
  • New certificate/diploma program/English Language School students: Please contact the ONECard Office to get a ONECard, then contact library staff to be registered for borrowing.

Extension students in all other extension courses are not currently able to borrow library materials.

Borrowing Information for University of Calgary, Faculty of Social Work

U of C Social Work undergraduate students may borrow library materials with a ONEcard. Please contact the ONECard Office to get a ONECard, then contact library staff to confirm you are registered for borrowing.

Picking up Books at Rutherford Library: you cannot book a time slot for curbside pick-up in the UAL system. After you receive your holds pick up notice by email, please proceed to Rutherford Library during curbside pickup hours and phone the number that is displayed on the Rutherford Library door.

Borrowing Information for Students from other Post-Secondary Institutions

Students from other Post-Secondary institutions who are living in Edmonton are elligible to borrow from NEOS member libraries. To inquire, please submit an Account Inquiry.

Borrowing information for TAL clients, Alumni, and Members of the Public

Unfortunately, due to COVID-19 restrictions, borrowing is not available for TAL clients, Alumni, and Members of the Public. Alumni patrons do have access to a subset of electronic materials through our Alumni portal.

Borrowing Information for University of Alberta Students, Academics & Staff and NEOS Members

Fines

We ask that you return items that have been recalled/are needed by other library users to the extent that it is possible. For help, please Ask Us, or fill out our fines appeal form to explain your situation. We understand that returns and meeting due dates are difficult during the pandemic, and we are limiting the application of fines.

Lost Charge: $150.00/item. At 31 days overdue, the item will be assumed lost and a lost fee of $150.00 will be applied. Please contact us via the Fines Appeal / Account Inquiry form if you have questions about charges on your library account.

We are working hard to adapt and update our borrowing services.

Thank you for your understanding.
