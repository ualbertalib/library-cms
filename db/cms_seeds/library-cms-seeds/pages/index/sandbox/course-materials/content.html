COVID-19 Access to Course Materials

COVID-19 Library Services

  • April 1 We are accepting Spring 2020 requests for course materials.
  • March 19 All course materials are closed to in-person use and holds are not available to be requested or picked up.

Winter 2020

During the pandemic, many textbook publishers have made the online versions of their textbooks available free of charge for students on the textbook platform VitalSource. Click this link to see the list of publishers. If you haven’t already, you’ll need to register for an account on VitalSource, and then search for your textbook, which you can then add to your VitalSource Bookshelf. Here’s a link to help with this process. For more help, you can click the Help button in the bottom-right corner of the page on VitalSource.

If you find your textbook on VitalSource, but it does not appear to be free of charge, you might need an access code, or further instructions, from your instructor.

If, after searching, you do not find your textbook on VitalSource at all, please submit a request to the Library using the Request for Digital Course Material form and we’ll help find an alternative as quickly as possible.

We also suggest that you let your instructor know if you have continued access issues.

Thank you for your patience as we investigate access options.
