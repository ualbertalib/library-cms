Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) Label in Bear Tracks

The Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) program is a student-lead initiative that empowers learners to make informed decisions about the cost of course materials at the point of course selection. Look for the "Zero Textbook Cost" filter in the Beartracks course registration software; this indicates students do not incur any costs for accessing required readings for this course.

Instructors are invited to self-declare their courses as ZTC to participate in this program.

Think outside the box! Alternatives to traditional commercial textbooks include:

  • Open Educational Resources (OER) and Open Access publications
  • Licensed library materials
  • Resources that are licensed in the public domain
  • Instructor-owned materials

To combine multiple open readings in a variety of formats, consider building a Reading List. If you are an instructor interested in exploring alternative course material options, contact oer.support@ualberta.ca for assistance.
