How can the Library help?

Librarians can help instructors by reviewing any relevant content licenses to ensure that what you're adopting is truly OER, or that changes you wish to make are allowed and that appropriate open licenses are applied to resulting products. If you're creating new OER, we can consult to help you choose the right license to make your work open and accessible, and we can help you publish your resource as a Pressbook through Open Education Alberta.

Reach out to our Library Publishing team at library.publishing@ualberta.ca

(Adapted from "How can the libraries help?", University of Texas Libraries, CC BY-NC.)

Creating, Adapting or Adopting OER

Publishing

Faculty and staff of the University of Alberta who want to create an OER for their class are eligible to participate in the Library's open textbook publishing program, which provides a no-fee publishing service for OER in partnership with Open Education Alberta.

Check out the Open Education Alberta Catalog to see publications produced by postsecondary institutions across Alberta, and contact library.publishing@ualberta.ca to see if our service is right for your learning materials.
