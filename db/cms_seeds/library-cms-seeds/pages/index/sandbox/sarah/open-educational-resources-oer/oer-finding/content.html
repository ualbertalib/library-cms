If you are interested in adopting an OER for your course and would like some help with finding appropriate options, contact oer.support@ualberta.ca for assistance.

Recommended

  • OER Commons - A public digital library of open educational resources
  • Open Textbook Library - Textbooks available through Center for Open Education, University of Minnesota.
  • LibreTexts - Online textbook platform collaboration between CA State University's Affordable Learning Solutions, US Dept. of Education, UC Davis, Merlot, & National Science Foundation
  • OpenStax - Peer-reviewed open educational textbooks published by Rice University
  • Merlot - CA State University System & Partners; provides access to curated online learning and support materials and content creation tools
  • Pressbooks Directory - Contains everything published in the Pressbooks platform; UAlberta Faculty and Staff have access to Pressbooks through Open Education Alberta so this is a good platform-specific search tool.
  • BCCampus - British Columbia Campus system's browse option for curated OER.
  • ECampus Ontario - Open Library of curated material from the Ontario campus system.
  • OERSI - A search index across multiple platforms; you may get a lot of results so try using both the keyword search and as many filters as you can apply.

Open Ancillary Materials (test banks, quizzes, slides, lesson plans etc.)

  • Open Stax Hub - A place for instructors to share ancillary materials they have built around Open Stax textbooks.
  • LibreTexts - Learning Objects can be searched within each discipline to share practice tools and assignments for learning.
  • PhET Interactive Simulations - Free interactive math and science simulations; K-12 and postsecondary with coverage in Physics, Biology, Mathematics, Chemistry and Earth Science.
  • ChemCollective (Chemistry) - Resources for teaching and learning chemistry at the undergraduate level. This includes virtual labs, tutorials, simulations, and more.

BC Campus Adoption Finder Tool

BC Campus maintains a list of Open Textbook adoptions. Consulting it is a quick way to see what resources other instructors are using. (Type Ctl + F to search by course title or code).
