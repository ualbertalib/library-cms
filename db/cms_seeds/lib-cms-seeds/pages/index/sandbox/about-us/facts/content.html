UofA Libraries Facts & Figures

Facts & Figures last updated May 2018.

Collections

print and electronic collections exceeding 5.2 million titles and over 7.5 million volumes including:

  • more than 120,000 scholarly ejournals across disciplines from a wide range of publishers
  • over 1.31 million ebooks accessible via a variety of formats
  • over 1,100 online databases
  • over 43,000 items in ERA: Education & Research Archive, UAlbertaâ€™s institutional repository
  • over 45 hosted open access journals
  • over 3,800 research data files available in Dataverse
  • more than 120,000 digitized books, 67,000 newspaper issues, and 20,000 images and maps related to the Canadian prairies and beyond

Spaces

Over 6,100 seats in 10 locations

  • over 700 Windows and over 150 Mac workstations , equipped with a wide range of browsers and office productivity software
  • over 85 bookable study and practice presentation rooms varied in size and setup designated common, quiet and silent areas, which have everything from individual carrels and comfortable soft seating to silent, technology free zones and large quiet reading rooms
  • a 24/7 study space, on the lower and first levels of Cameron Library
  • 98 PC laptops, 41 MacBook, 4 digital cameras, and 2 video cameras available for loan
  • 5 3D printers available for student use, in partnership with the Faculty of Science

Annually

  • over 480,000 circulations
  • over 13,000 items borrowed via ILL for UAL users and almost 35,000 items lent via ILL to external users
  • open 168 hours per week fall/spring term
  • 2.8 million visitors to our physical libraries
  • over 2.5 million views of UAL home page
  • over 120,000 research questions answered via our physical and virtual service points
  • over 680 classes taught to more than 21,500 students, faculty, staff, and community members
