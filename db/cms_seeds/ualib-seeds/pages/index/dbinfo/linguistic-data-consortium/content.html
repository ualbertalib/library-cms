Linguistic Data Consortium

Click here for access

The LDC is an open consortium of universities, companies and government research laboratories hosted by the University of Pennsylvania, which creates, collects and distributes speech and text databases, lexicons, and other resources for research and development purposes. The LDC's catalogue contains hundreds of corpora of language data. In addition, the LDC Online contains an indexed collection of Arabic, Chinese and English newswire text, millions of words of English telephone speech from the Switchboard and Fisher collections and the American English Spoken Lexicon, as well as the full text of the Brown corpus. Using LDC Online, users can search textual data and play audio extracts for transcribed utterances on standard web browsers.

The University of Alberta is an institutional member of the Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC). Access is available to all language corpora in the LDC Catalog from 2006 forward and to selected corpora published between 1996 and 2005. LDC content is available in various physical formats (CDs, DVDs, hard drives, etc.) as well as online for more recent corpora:

1. Physical Formats: To request access to individual language corpora, please fill in the Data Help Request form and you will be contacted with information on where to access the material. Check the Library Catalogue to search for individual corpora. SEARCH TIP: In the Library search box type ‘LDC’ and your language of interest.

2. LDC-Online: Selected corpora can be accessed online through the LDC website, which requires an individual account. To begin, first sign up for a free LDC user account. Once your account is approved by the Library's LDC Membership Administrator, you will be notified that you have been granted full member access.

Access LDC-Online (Note: When creating your user account, make sure that you select ‘University of Alberta Libraries’ from the ‘Organization’ drop-down menu).

For assistance, please email: libldc@ualberta.ca
